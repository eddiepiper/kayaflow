# Pattern: Buried Risk Disclosure

## Context
When important risk information is placed below the fold, in a footer, in a modal, or in legal fine print — separated from the benefit claim or CTA it qualifies.

## Why It Matters
Buried risk disclosures are functionally invisible to most users. They do not serve their purpose of informing the user before a decision. In financial products, this creates both regulatory risk and customer trust risk — especially if a user later claims they were not adequately informed.

## Good Example
Risk information appearing inline, in plain language, adjacent to the relevant figure or benefit — not requiring the user to scroll, tap, or seek it out.

## Bad Example
"Important notice: Returns are not guaranteed. Capital is at risk." appearing in 8pt text in a grey footer, three screens after the benefit was first displayed.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
For any screen showing financial benefits, returns, or principal claims: check whether corresponding risk disclosure is visible in the same viewport without scrolling. Flag anything that requires user action to find.
