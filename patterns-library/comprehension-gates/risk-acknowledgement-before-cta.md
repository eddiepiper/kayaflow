# Pattern: Risk Acknowledgement Before CTA

## Context
When a CTA ("Invest Now", "Proceed", "Subscribe") appears on a screen where the user has not yet had an opportunity to acknowledge key risks, conditions, or product mechanics.

## Why It Matters
For financial products, a CTA that advances commitment before comprehension is established creates legal, ethical, and trust risk. Users may proceed without understanding what they are agreeing to. A comprehension gate — even a simple checkbox — creates a moment of deliberate acknowledgement.

## Good Example
A checkbox: "I understand that returns are not guaranteed and that principal may be at risk" — required to be checked before the "Proceed" CTA becomes active.

## Bad Example
"Invest Now" button active from screen load, with risk disclosure appearing in a separate section the user has not scrolled to.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
For investment, structured deposit, or commitment screens, check whether the CTA requires any form of risk acknowledgement. Flag CTAs that are immediately active with no acknowledgement gate on high-commitment screens.
