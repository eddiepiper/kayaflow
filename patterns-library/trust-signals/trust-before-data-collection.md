# Pattern: Trust Before Data Collection

## Context
When an onboarding or KYC flow asks for sensitive personal data (NRIC, income, employment details) before explaining why it is needed or what it will be used for.

## Why It Matters
Users are risk-averse with personal data. Asking for sensitive information before establishing context or explaining purpose creates unnecessary friction and abandonment risk. Trust must be earned before commitment is requested.

## Good Example
"We need your NRIC to verify your identity and comply with MAS regulations. Your information is encrypted and never shared with third parties." — shown before the NRIC input field.

## Bad Example
NRIC input field as the first element on the screen with no explanation.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
When reviewing KYC or onboarding screens, check whether sensitive data fields are preceded by a clear explanation of purpose and a reassurance message. Flag missing context before high-friction data collection.
