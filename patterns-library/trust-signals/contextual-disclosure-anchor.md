# Pattern: Contextual Disclosure Anchor

## Context
When a financial product screen shows a return figure, interest rate, or principal protection claim without an adjacent qualifying condition.

## Why It Matters
Users anchor on the most prominent number they see. If the condition qualifying that number (e.g., "up to", "subject to bank solvency") is separated or below the fold, the user may treat a conditional return as guaranteed. This creates overconfidence before a financial commitment.

## Good Example
"3.37% p.a. (maximum potential return, not guaranteed)" displayed inline beside the rate figure, with a plain-language note directly beneath explaining conditions.

## Bad Example
"Earn 3.37% total." in large text at the top of the screen, with a footnote at the bottom of the page referencing conditions in legal copy.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
Look for rate figures, return claims, or principal protection statements. Check whether the qualifying condition is inline and proximate, or separated into footer or scroll-below content. Flag if the benefit is prominent and the condition is not.
