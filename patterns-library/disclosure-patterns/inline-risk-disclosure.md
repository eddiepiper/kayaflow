# Pattern: Inline Risk Disclosure

## Context
When risk disclosures are placed only in a footer, a separate legal tab, or a modal that requires user action to access — rather than inline near the relevant claim.

## Why It Matters
Footer-only disclosures are routinely ignored. For financial products, critical risk information placed away from the decision point fails its purpose. Inline placement ensures the user sees the risk in the same context as the benefit.

## Good Example
A structured deposit screen showing: "3.37% p.a. maximum return" with a directly adjacent inline note: "Capital is at risk. Returns are not guaranteed and depend on underlying performance."

## Bad Example
A benefit-led headline followed by a small asterisk, with risk disclosure appearing only in fine print below a fold or in a terms modal.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
Check whether risk language appears proximate to the benefit claim on the same screen viewport. Flag disclosures that are only in footers, modals, or require scrolling to reach.
