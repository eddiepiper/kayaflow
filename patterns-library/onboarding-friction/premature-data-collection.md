# Pattern: Premature Data Collection

## Context
When an onboarding flow requests sensitive or high-friction data (income, employment, NRIC, tax residency) before the user has understood the product, its benefits, or why the data is needed.

## Why It Matters
Asking for high-friction data too early in a flow creates abandonment risk. Users who have not yet formed a reason to trust the product or the brand are more likely to exit when confronted with sensitive data requests. Motivation and context must precede commitment.

## Good Example
A flow that explains the product, shows benefits, establishes eligibility, and only then requests identity or income verification — with a clear explanation of why it is needed.

## Bad Example
NRIC or income fields as the second screen in an onboarding flow, before the user has seen what they are signing up for.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
In onboarding and KYC screens, check the ordering of information versus data collection. Flag screens where high-friction data is requested before product context or trust has been established.
