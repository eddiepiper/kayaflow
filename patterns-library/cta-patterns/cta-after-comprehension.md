# Pattern: CTA After Comprehension

## Context
When the primary CTA appears at the top of the screen or before the user has seen the key conditions, risks, or product mechanics relevant to the decision.

## Why It Matters
CTA placement signals to the user how much deliberation is expected. A CTA at the top of a complex product screen communicates "this is easy, just proceed" — which may not match the actual complexity or risk of the action. CTA timing should match the seriousness of the next step.

## Good Example
A product detail screen that walks through: what the product is, what the return is, what the conditions are, what the risks are — and then presents the CTA at the bottom after the user has had an opportunity to read the key information.

## Bad Example
"Get Started" as the first prominent element on a structured deposit screen, with product details appearing below.

## KayaFlow Review Cue
Check where on the screen the primary CTA appears relative to key product information. Flag if the CTA appears above or before critical conditions, risks, or comprehension content.
