# Banking Trust Patterns

## Key Risks
- Overconfidence from benefit-led copy
- Risk disclosures appearing too late
- Legal text buried below decision points
- "Up to" return wording misunderstood as guaranteed return
- Principal protection misunderstood as unconditional
- Users progressing before comprehension

## Senior Reviewer Rules
- Benefits and risks must be balanced before decision points
- Important conditions should be near the claim they qualify
- CTA should not appear before comprehension is reasonably established
- Do not rewrite regulated claims as definitive unless confirmed
- Flag ambiguity instead of assuming intent

## Example

Weak:
"Earn 3.37% total."

Better:
"Clarify whether 3.37% is guaranteed, conditional, or maximum potential return."

## Trust Signal Patterns
- Inline contextual disclosure
- Risk acknowledgement before CTA
- Clear distinction between guaranteed, conditional, and illustrative returns
- Plain-language explanation before legal disclosure
